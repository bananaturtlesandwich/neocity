#all
this is my log! a relatively exhaustive list of media i've consumed in a roughly chronological order and my thoughts on them. i will only put media on here that i think is worth talking about so i won't put any generic novels or mediocre shovelware here. it is by no means complete and will continue to grow as long as i continue to be a huge fucking nerd :p
#games
i love games - they combine all different techniques and can incorporate story, art, interactivity, worldbuilding, music and so much else. because you take control you can really immerse yourself in a world or you can just play a simple little puzzle game. both are valid. although i fucking hate microtransactions. fuck that.
#wii
the wii was my first console. i got it as a birthday present when i was around six but didn't play it much until years later. i think the simplicity of the wii-mote and novelty of motion controls removed a lot of mental tax in control schemes but also felt a bit limiting for more technical games
#gameboy
my dad had a gameboy which has now been passed on ceremoniously to me. despite how miniscule the screen is, it is such a charming little handheld console. i'm a big fan of the tiny sprites and chirpy audio and have found a bunch of good cartridges for it from CEX... although i do also have a bunch of shovelware on it from my dad
#3DS
my mum actually never knew i had a 3DS. since she wasn't often home due to business trips, my dad got me a 3DS which she wouldn't have been very happy with because she used to be quite strict. i managed to get a bunch of games for it from my cousin (who sucks). the 3D view is really awesome i wish more consoles had that feature.
#PC
i didn't really think of the pc as a platform until i got steam during the pandemic. but as soon as i got into modding i suddenly had a lot more interest in getting games on the pc (even if my computer specifications aren't exactly up to scratch). i also have a bunch of freebies from epic and amazon games (which a lot of people literally have no idea exists)
#switch
i got my switch for christmas in the year of the pandemic. it's so convenient to have by virtue of being a hybrid console and being able to play handheld and on the big screen. i also have an industrial-grade travel case for it so it can go anywhere i do
#3D platformer
3D platformers might be my favourite game genre at the moment. the extra axis of movement introduces so much freedom, lending itself to larger spaces and incredible variety in movesets
#2D platformer
although the pool of 2D platformer games is full of baby's first games and a shit ton of shovelware, there really is some gems in the genre which you can find. these games can still be very diverse in movesets with enough creativity from developers.
#arcade
just simple little games where you can start playing and try to get high score
#metroidvania
the focus on exploration and ability-based progression makes metroidvanias very cool to me. they lend themselves to large, interconnected worlds which have incredibly deep lore which is always so fucking cool. but nowadays the genre is so saturated that standing out is extremely difficult.
#rpg
honestly, an rpg really has to be special to appeal to me. it's not normally my type of game but there is some which slipped through the cracks
#roguelike
i think the reason i don't play more roguelikes is because they have infinite replayability so would take up a lot of my time if i had more in my library. although a lot of roguelikes don't have enough variety to stay fresh and fun.
#mario
mario is such a goofy property. my favourite games in the series are the 3D platformers but that's a genre bias. there's so many bloody spinoffs it's crazy, from olympics with sonic, tennis, american football, typing and doctoring what can't mario do? and of course there's the iconic tv series and crisp rat movie...
#zelda
i love the zelda franchise for its sense of adventure, its deep lore and its puzzle box dungeons. breath of the wild and tears of the kingdom took the genre in the open-world direction. i didn't really like that in the process they chose to dumb down the dungeons and make the world empty (since there is such a massive area to fill with content)
#pokemon
i do love the world of pokemon. so many awesome creatures with their own (often dark) origins. the battle system hold a surprising amount of depth and battles can be fun to strategise around when well designed. personally, i don't think the mainline games have been good since sun and moon and haven't played any past it. the move to switch meant a doubled price for a buggier game of the same scope and DLC that cost the same. while i like some of the newer designs most don't really do it for me. fangames are where the innovation is happening and i firmly believe this will continue to be the case.
#films
films and cinema are very special. for that hour to two hours of screentime (yes that's how long films should be) to stick with you the film-makers need to do a damn good job. the acting, cinematography, effects and sound design all work together to communicate stories and experiences.
#shows
shows are very much in the vain of films but have even more oppurtunity for character development and worldbuilding because of the drastic increase in screentime. they can be fun and episodic but in terms of show storytelling, serialisation is the best way to go (or a hybrid like teen titans)
#books
although books lack the visual and audio aspect, this can be a strength. when reading, your mind creates its own details and idea of what characters look like or how they act which may contrast completely with someone else's interpretation. you can imagine scenes which would be technically extremely challenging and costly to make on screen. this can make worlds feel more personal because nobody perceives it how you have in your head.
#podcasts
much like books, podcasts only use a single sense in audio, which means worlds and characters are open to the interpretation of your mind. i'm not much of a podcast listener but horror/thriller stories really lend themselves to this as you can't see the threat but you can hear it.

this was my first zelda game!

when i played it i didn't realise this was considered the best game in the zelda series at the time, even the best game period by many since i just got the cartridge from my cousin but i certainly support that opinion now

the game is endlessly full of life and care - the world, characters, environments, lore, enemies and music are

the world has such rich cultures from the earlier games and the 3D environments really enhance their homes: the gorons have their city carved into the base of a volcano; the zora made their home in an idyllic cave under lake hylia; the kokiri dwell in the vibrant lost lost woods; and the hylians have towns dotted all over hyrule

the story is surprisingly compelling for a gameplay focussed experience! the deku tree "gifts" you with navi and charges you with purging darkness from him. after, you set off into the world to warn the kingdom of the evil which turns out to be the king of thieves by taking the sacred stones yourself you stop ganondorf getting them which leads you to the two other early dungeons. however this backfires when you open the doors to the sacred realm and ganondorf takes the triforce. you must find the sages as an adult who are the only ones with the power to seal him away leading to the rest of the dungeons. i love this twist so much since the ganondorf's success changes enemies, characters and locations in the future and you experience disastrous his rule has been for the kingdom firsthand. i also remember being so shocked yet delighted by this since i expected the game to end after only the three dungeons but the realisation that was only the tutorial was magical.

the dungeons themselves are genuinely fantastic and i cannot fault a single one of them. each have such great context behind them which make them a joy to explore - i especially love the aesthetics of jabu-jabu's belly and the forest, shadow and spirit temples. of course the puzzles themselves aren't always head-scratchers but that was never the intent. the exploration of the places and the bosses are where zelda dungeons shine. i love the bosses and each one of them is amazing.

i played the 3ds version so didn't suffer the iron boots pain since i could map them to a button like the other items. the touch controls i think were integrated quite nicely in this respect, adding some more slots to map items. it's such a great system which i'm baffled by the choice to remove since breath of the wild.

oh my god. the music. ocarina of time's soundtrack might be my favourite soundtrack in any game ever. the simple melodies are some of the most iconic and memorable i have ever heard and every now and then i'll find myself humming them. since they had to be playable on the ocarina this even limited the palette of notes which many of the tunes used. however this certainly didn't stop zelda's theme, lon lon ranch, the lost woods and the windmill hut from being bangers. when unrestrained the melodies can really let loose - the title theme, kakariko village, great fairy fountain, gerudo valley and kotake and koume live rent-free in my brain. but even the ambient tunes are so immediately recognisable and whenever used in youtube videos i always catch house, kokiri forest, shop and hyrule field

now i'm thinking about it mario galaxy was probably my first non arcade game i ever played

i don't think we've had a mario adventure of such scale since galaxy - the bombastic music, the planetoids, the vastness of space and the proportion of bossfights are uncomparable (yes galaxy 2 exists but i think the world map detracts from it). the gravity mechanics are a fun gimmick which play well with a bunch of other mechanics

galaxy was the first 3D mario to have worlds which were purely linear which i'm slightly disappointed by. my disappointment mostly stems from the fact that the less open-ended levels meant galaxy limited mario's moveset. it really is a stark difference from odyssey's vast pool of moves - mario can jump, sideflip, backflip, longjump and spin and that's it. the spin allows for some cool sequence breaks but there isn't much beyond that. otherwise levels are pretty scripted in terms of how you can approach obstacles.

the power ups are fun - bee mushroom allows climbing on honeycombs and limited flight, boo mushroom is infinite flight and intangibility, spring mushroom gives big jump but limits movement, koopa shell boosts swimming and fire and ice flowers do what you expect. they add some gameplay variety and are used sporadically so don't overstay their welcome. i think my favourite is the ice flower because it completely recontextualises water in the environment - you can skate on ponds, fountains become platforms and even waterfalls can be walljumped.

the motion control games on the other hand can go fuck themselves

there's literally an entire motion control set of galaxies which were are a slog to get through. ball balancing, manta surfing and bubble blowing aren't bad just slow to control but the amount of levels dedicated to them and how long they are is what pisses me off.

the proper levels are so good though! the imagination put into each galaxy is brilliant and some mechanics really put a smile on your face. i loved disassembling toy bowser, climbing up buoy base, soaring with dandelions and so many more things. the boss fights are so well done as well! they don't feel similar whatsoever despite all having the three-hit pattern (apart from bonefin who was just terrifying). the endgame stuff can get pretty intense and awesome too with purple comets. the purple comets are great because they open up the entire galaxy, merge levels together in a galaxy finale or create some new terrifying gauntlet.

but the levels get soured in full completion with the other comets...

prankster comets essentially just recycle content artificially with modifiers. one hit death, time limit, racing, faster enemies, whatever. all it does is bloat the already massive amount of content - they're an eighth of the content and the main reason i'm reluctant to do the full completion with luigi
